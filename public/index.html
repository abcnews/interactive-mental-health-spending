Wed 19 Aug 2020, 4:49 PM AEST

If there’s one thing we can all agree on, it’s that 2020 has been a rough year. It started with the country in flames before shifting from one apocalyptic scenario to the next as coronavirus reached our shores.

With Australia staring down a “mental health pandemic”, it’s worth asking, is our system equipped to cope?

Besides landing in an emergency department, most Australians access mental health care through the Medicare system.

You might be one of the approximately 1.32 million Australians who’ve been given a mental health plan by your GP - a piece of paper that opens up Medicare-rebated sessions with psychologists and other professionals. Or you may have seen a psychiatrist in private practice.

Taxpayers are spending $1.3 billion each year on Medicare rebates for mental health care, so it’s time we asked - is help getting to those who need it most?

We’re about to show you some fancy charts, but to make them more relevant for you we’ll need your postcode, or a postcode you’re curious about. Don’t worry, we’re not storing or re-using this information.

Is your area accessing care?

This year, we’ve all become experts in reading curvy lines. But we’re about to show you a curve you might not have seen before.

This line tracks the proportion of people experiencing a high level of psychological distress. On the left we have the least disadvantaged fifth of Australians and on the right we have the most disadvantaged.
